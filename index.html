Tyre (Sour), Lebanon

A coastal city famous for wide sandy beaches and rich archaeological heritage. This page highlights key areas, beaches, and how to reach Tyre — plus a quick way to contact us.

Explore beaches Send a message
Beach vibes Historic sites Food & cafés

Why visit?

Mix a beach day with ruins, old streets, cafés, and sunset views by the sea.

Best season

Late spring to early autumn for swimming. Winter is perfect for calm walks.

Local name

Locals commonly say Sour (صور). You’ll see both “Tyre” and “Sour”.

Popular Areas

Good starting points for walking, food, history, and sea views.

Al-Mina (peninsula & harbor vibe)

  • Seaside walks and cafés
  • Coastal views and relaxing evenings
  • Great for photos at sunset

El-Bass (archaeological zone)

  • Major ruins area
  • Great morning visit in summer
  • Comfortable shoes recommended

Old streets & local markets

  • Local shops and snacks
  • Nice evening stroll
  • Authentic city feel

Ras El Ain area

  • Quieter vibe
  • Green spots and calm breaks
  • Nice stop on the way in/out

Beaches

From wide sandy stretches to lively summer spots.

Nature-style sandy coast

  • Long walks on the sand
  • Quieter atmosphere
  • Perfect for sunrise / sunset

Public beach strip

  • Busy in summer
  • Good for groups
  • Arrive early on weekends

Seaside rocks & viewpoints

  • Best for photos
  • Sea breeze and relaxing vibes
  • Great for winter walks too

Beach + history combo

  • Morning: ruins
  • Afternoon: beach
  • Evening: food & cafés

How to reach Tyre

Quick route buttons + typical driving approach.

By car

  • Fastest and most convenient option
  • Routes typically pass through Saida (Sidon) depending on traffic
  • Plan extra time on summer weekends
Open Beirut → Tyre in Google Maps Open Tyre in OpenStreetMap

By service / shared taxi

  • Availability depends on time and season
  • Ask for “Sour / صور” as destination
  • Confirm drop-off point (center, beach, or hotel)
